1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used across the web to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to site owners about how their website is being used.

Cookies are set by the website you are visiting (known as "first-party cookies") or by third-party services used by that website (known as "third-party cookies"). They are stored in your browser and sent back to the originating website on subsequent visits, allowing the website to recognise your device and recall relevant information.

Session cookies are temporary files that exist only for the duration of your browsing session. They are deleted from your device as soon as you close your browser. Session cookies are primarily used to maintain the continuity of your experience as you navigate between pages — for example, keeping you logged in or remembering items in a form as you progress through it.

Persistent cookies remain on your device after your browser is closed and survive until their expiry date or until you manually delete them. These cookies are used to remember your preferences between visits, to recognise you as a returning visitor, and to collect analytics data over time. The expiry period for persistent cookies varies — we specify the duration for each cookie we use in the tables below.

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons (small transparent image files), pixel tags, and local storage objects. These technologies function similarly to cookies and may be used in conjunction with them. For simplicity, all such technologies are referred to as "cookies" in this policy.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for a number of specific, legitimate purposes that help us provide you with a better experience on our website and understand how the site is being used. We do not use cookies to collect sensitive personal information, to track your activity across unrelated websites, or for behavioural advertising.

Ensuring website functionality: Some cookies are strictly necessary for the website to operate correctly. Without these, certain features — such as maintaining your session or protecting forms against cross-site request forgery — would not work. These essential cookies are placed automatically and do not require your consent.

Understanding how you use our site: Analytics cookies help us understand visitor behaviour — which pages are most popular, how users navigate between sections, where they enter and exit the site, and how long they spend on different content. This information is collected in an anonymised or aggregated form and is used to improve the website experience for all users.

Remembering your preferences: If you interact with preference controls on our website (such as accepting or declining non-essential cookies), we use a cookie to remember your choice so you are not asked again on your next visit. This is itself an essential function of the consent management mechanism.

We regularly review which cookies are in use on our site to ensure we are only collecting data that is genuinely necessary or beneficial, and that all cookies align with our data protection obligations under POPIA.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing how cookies are used when you visit our website. We respect your choices and want to make it as easy as possible for you to control your cookie experience.

Browser settings: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to: block all cookies; accept all cookies; block third-party cookies only; delete existing cookies; or be notified when a new cookie is set. Please refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions — the exact steps vary between browsers and versions. Common browsers and their cookie control pages are listed below:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and stored data

Opt-out tools: For analytics cookies, you can opt out of tracking by Google Analytics using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. You can also use the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page or the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out tool to manage interest-based advertising cookies from participating companies.

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled without impacting your ability to use the site. Your cookie preferences are saved using a small cookie — if you delete all cookies from your browser, your preferences will be reset and you may be asked to confirm them again on your next visit.

In accordance with POPIA, we will seek your consent before placing any non-essential cookies on your device. If you subsequently wish to withdraw your consent, you may do so by adjusting your browser settings or contacting us, though withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

7. Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics (GA4) to collect anonymised data about how visitors use mapengo.co.za. Google Analytics helps us answer questions like: Which pages are most visited? We do not use Google Analytics data to create individual user profiles or for targeted advertising. Analytics data is used solely in aggregate to understand website performance. You can opt out of Google Analytics at any time using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on or by adjusting your cookie preferences.

We periodically assess whether our use of analytics cookies is proportionate to the insights gained, and we will reduce or discontinue their use if we find simpler, less intrusive methods available to meet our legitimate interests.
