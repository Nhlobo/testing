1. Introduction

Mapengo Innovations (Pty) Ltd ("Mapengo", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information with the care and transparency it deserves. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose personal information when you visit our website at mapengo.co.za, interact with us via email or phone, or engage us to provide professional services.

We operate in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("POPIA"), South Africa's primary data protection legislation. POPIA sets out conditions for the lawful processing of personal information and grants individuals specific rights over their data. Where applicable, we also comply with other relevant legislation including the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 ("ECTA").

This policy applies to all personal information we collect about you, whether through our website, direct correspondence, service agreements, or any other interaction with Mapengo Innovations. It does not apply to third-party websites or services that may be linked from our site — please review the privacy policies of those platforms independently.

We may update this policy from time to time. Any material changes will be communicated through our website, and the effective date at the top of this document will be revised accordingly. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information only where it is necessary for a legitimate purpose and always with your awareness. The categories of information we may collect include:

Information you provide directly: When you complete a contact form, request a quote, or engage our services, you may provide your full name, email address, phone number, company name, job title, and a description of your project or requirements. We collect only what is reasonably necessary to respond to your enquiry or deliver our services.

Service-related information: In the course of delivering digital, technology, or creative services, we may process additional business information shared by you, including project briefs, business documents, system credentials (managed under strict security protocols), and communications related to service delivery. This information is used solely for the purpose for which it was shared.

Automatically collected data: When you browse our website, we may automatically collect technical information such as your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, time spent on pages, and interaction data. This information is collected via cookies and analytics tools and helps us understand how visitors use our site so we can improve the user experience.

Communications: If you contact us by email, phone, or through our website, we retain records of that communication, including your contact details and the content of your message, to enable us to respond effectively and maintain a record of our interactions.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect for specific, legitimate purposes. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

Responding to enquiries: When you contact us or submit a project enquiry, we use your information to respond to your query, provide a quote or proposal, and follow up appropriately. This is the primary reason most visitors share their details with us.

Service delivery: For clients who engage our services, we use your personal and project information to plan, deliver, and manage the agreed scope of work. This may include coordinating with our team, subcontractors, or tools necessary to complete your project.

Communications and relationship management: We may use your contact information to send you updates about your project, respond to support requests, or share relevant information about our services. We will only send marketing communications where you have opted in, and you can unsubscribe at any time.

Website improvement and analytics: Anonymised and aggregated usage data helps us identify how visitors navigate our site, which content is most useful, and where we can improve the user experience. This data does not identify you personally.

Legal and compliance obligations: We may process your information where required to meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, or respond to lawful requests from public authorities.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (POPIA)

Under POPIA, we are required to have a lawful basis — referred to as a "justification ground" — for processing your personal information. We rely on the following grounds depending on the context:

Contractual necessity: Where you have engaged us to provide services or are in the process of entering into a service agreement, we process your personal information as necessary to fulfil that contract or to take pre-contractual steps at your request.

Legitimate interest: We may process your personal information where we have a legitimate business interest, such as improving our website, managing our client relationships, and ensuring the security of our systems. We balance our legitimate interests against your rights and will not override your interests or fundamental freedoms.

Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process personal information — for example, to send you marketing communications or to place non-essential cookies — you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

Legal obligation: We may process your information where we are legally required to do so, such as complying with tax legislation, court orders, or regulatory obligations imposed by competent South African authorities.

As the responsible party under POPIA, Mapengo Innovations (Pty) Ltd determines the purposes and means of processing personal information. Our designated Information Officer is responsible for overseeing our compliance with POPIA and managing any data-related enquiries or complaints.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We treat your personal information with strict confidentiality and do not disclose it to third parties except in the limited circumstances described below.

Service providers and operators: We may share your information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our business, such as cloud hosting providers, email platforms, project management tools, and payment processors. These parties act as "operators" under POPIA and are bound by contractual obligations to protect your data and use it only for the specific purposes we have authorised.

Professional advisers: We may share information with our legal, financial, or accounting advisers where necessary and where they are bound by professional confidentiality obligations.

Legal requirements: We may disclose your personal information if required to do so by law, court order, or at the request of a competent public authority, including the South African Police Service, the South African Revenue Service, or any other regulatory body with lawful authority.

Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets involving Mapengo Innovations, personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify affected individuals before their information is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

We do not disclose personal information to third parties for their own marketing purposes. Any sharing of data is conducted on a need-to-know basis, limited to what is strictly necessary for the stated purpose.

6. Data Security

We take the security of your personal information seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or destruction. These measures are commensurate with the sensitivity of the information and the risks involved.

Technical safeguards include the use of HTTPS/TLS encryption for data transmission, secure password management practices, restricted access controls based on the principle of least privilege, and regular security assessments of our systems and third-party tools.

Organisational safeguards include staff awareness of data protection obligations, confidentiality undertakings for anyone who accesses personal information in the course of their work, and documented procedures for handling personal information securely.

Despite these measures, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. In the event of a data security compromise that poses a risk to you, we will notify you and the Information Regulator as required by POPIA within a reasonable timeframe.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Our retention practices are guided by the following principles:

Client and project records are retained for a minimum of five (5) years after the conclusion of the engagement to meet our tax and contractual obligations, and to address any disputes or claims that may arise. Financial records may be retained for longer periods as required by the South African Revenue Service.

Enquiries and correspondence that do not progress to a service engagement are retained for a period of up to twelve (12) months, after which they are securely deleted or anonymised unless you request earlier deletion.

Website analytics data is typically retained for twenty-six (26) months, in line with standard analytics platform settings, after which it is anonymised or deleted.

When personal information is no longer required, we take reasonable steps to securely delete or anonymise it so that it can no longer be associated with you. You may request early deletion of your personal information subject to any overriding legal or contractual obligations — see Section 8 for your rights in this regard.

8. Your Rights Under POPIA

POPIA grants you a number of rights in relation to the personal information we hold about you. We are committed to upholding these rights and will respond to valid requests within a reasonable timeframe, and in any event within thirty (30) days of receipt unless a longer period is justified.

Right of access (Section 23): You have the right to request confirmation of whether we hold personal information about you, and to receive a copy of that information. We may require you to verify your identity before releasing any personal information.

Right to correction or deletion (Section 24): You may request that we correct inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive, out-of-date, incomplete, misleading, or unlawfully obtained personal information. Where deletion is warranted and not precluded by our legal obligations, we will securely delete your information.

Right to object (Section 11(3)): You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information on grounds relating to your particular situation, where we rely on legitimate interest as our lawful basis. You also have the absolute right to object to the processing of your personal information for direct marketing purposes at any time.

Right to restriction: In certain circumstances, you may request that we restrict the processing of your personal information — for example, while we verify the accuracy of information you have contested.

Right to lodge a complaint: If you believe we have processed your personal information unlawfully or in breach of POPIA, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa. Their contact details are: JD House, 27 Stiemens Street, Braamfontein, Johannesburg 2001 | complaints.IR@justice.gov.za | +27 10 023 5200.

To exercise any of your rights, please contact our Information Officer using the details in Section 13. We may ask you to provide proof of identity to process your request and to protect against fraudulent requests.

9. Cookies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyse website traffic, and support our marketing activities. A cookie is a small text file placed on your device when you visit a website. For a detailed breakdown of the cookies we use, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

Essential cookies are required for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as session management, security, and accessibility. These cookies are placed without requiring your consent as they are strictly necessary for the delivery of the service you have requested.

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting anonymised usage data. We use Google Analytics for this purpose. These cookies are only placed with your consent, where a consent mechanism is in place.

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse new cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when new cookies are set. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website.
